NEPAL

Nepal is the country residing in the heart of himalayas, simply blessed with 8 out of 10 highest mountains. Nepal, also known as Switzerland of Asia, is a small landlocked country full of exciting sceneries. Experience of being in this "Heaven on earth" will provide rejuvenating, enjoyable,peaceful and exciting sensations. Nepal's diverse geography includes the lustrous forest of Terai and the blissful gifts from nature that reside in the hills.

Nepal is the home to more than 125 ethnic groups that have rewarded the country with lots of colorful festivals, cultures, tasty foods and ancient architecture.

KATHMANDU UNIVERSITY

Kathmandu University is an autonomous, not-for-profit, non-government institution dedicated to maintaining high standards of academic excellence. It is committed to develop leaders in professional areas through quality education. It is located in a mountainous landscape in Dhulikhel Municipality about 30 kilometers east to Kathmandu having round-the-year pleasant climate and panoramic Himalayan views. Within a period of 25 years, Kathmandu University has built not only reasonable infrastructure, but also established a track record of academic excellence. At present the university offers various undergraduate, graduate and postgraduate programs in science, engineering, medical, management, education, arts, pharmacy, environment, music, human and natural resources, information technology, biotechnology and law.

DEPARTMENT OF PHARMACY

The Department of Pharmacy at Kathmandu University was established in 1994 with a mission to cater and supply a steady stream of highly competent pharmacy graduates required for the country and to contribute knowledge in pharmacy and pharmaceutical sciences. Since then, it has grown to become one of the leading Pharmacy Departments in Nepal. It started BPharm program in1994, MPharm program in 2000, PhD in 2004 and Pharm D (Post Baccalaureate) in 2010 all for the first time in Nepal. Department is currently running MPharm in two specializations: Industrial Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Care.

FORUM FOR PHARMACY

Forum For Pharmacy (FoP), an organization of pharmacy students studying at Kathmandu University (KU), was Established in 1999 by the pharmacy students of KU for the welfare of pharmacy education and the profession as a whole with an objective to work for professional and academic activity in and out of KU. It is totally a Non-Political and a Non-Profit organization. Forum For Pharmacy has been actively involved in carrying out activities in order to promote Public Health Through Provision Of Information, Education, Networking And A Range Of Publications And Professional Initiatives. Being A Neutral Organization, It Has Been Working For The Social Benefit And There Is No Any Place For Affiliation, Sympathy Or Any Political Activities In The Organization. Since The Organization Is Independent To Organize Its Activities Within The Limit Of Its Objective Under The Umbrella Of KU, FoP Has Organized Different Professional And Academic Activities Like Basic Health Skills Training, Health Awareness Programs, Supporting National Campaigns, Pharmacy Education Programs, Leadership Trainings, Seminars, Documentaries Screenings, Etc. Besides These FoP Is Also Working For The Organization Of National And International Conferences And Symposiums, Alumni Meet, Professional Development Projects And Others.

IPSF (International Pharmaceutical Students' Federation)

The International Pharmaceutical Students’ Federation (IPSF) was founded in 1949 in London, United Kingdom. The IPSF Secretariat is supported and hosted by the FIP in The Hague, The Netherlands. The federation now represents approximately 500,000 pharmacy students and recent graduates in more than 90 countries worldwide. IPSF has 5 regional offices led by Regional Working Group (RWG) in the Pan American, Europe, Middle East, Asia-Pacific, and Africa. IPSF is the leading international advocacy organization for pharmacy students promoting improved public health through provision of information, education, networking, and a range of publications and professional activities.

IPSF initiatives focus mainly on the areas of public health, pharmacy education, and professional development. Initiatives include public health campaigns, research on issues in pharmacy education and workforce development, the Student Exchange Program, organizing international and regional congresses and symposia, and publications of the IPSF News Bulletin, Newsletters and educational supplement (Phuture).

IPSF APRO (Asia Pacific Regional Office)

Founded in 1999, IPSF APRO is the oldest regional office that represents pharmacy students and recent graduates in Asia-Pacific countries. It is composed of diverse culture, languages and Pharmaceutical societies of 18 countries, 28 member associations  and more than 70000 members . IPSF APRO focuses to develop and serve the needs of the region by composing different approaches to public health, professional development, and pharmacy education. IPSF APRO produces the quality of future healthcare professionals by providing our members with equipped professional skills in industrial, clinical, community, and research development of the region.  

The IPSF APRO Good Pharmacy Practice Education (GPPed) Program is one of the special regional events only held by the region. This event focuses on developing and standardizing the pharmacy practice skills of our members. . The 6th GPPed event is going to be hosted by Forum For Pharmacy (FoP), Nepal .

ABOUT GPPed

Good Pharmacy Practice

Medicines are an essential but also a critical part of health-care services in all cultures and societies. Faulty or counterfeit medicines are a serious health hazard. Patient adherence, interactions and costs are other difficulties associated with medicines. Pharmacists play a major role in easing such difficulties. GPP stands for Good Pharmacy Practice, which aims to improve pharmacy services offered to the general public. GPP is the practice of pharmacy that responds to the needs of the people who use the pharmacists’ services to provide optimal, evidence-based care. To support this practice it is essential that there be an established national framework of quality standards and guidelines.

GPPed (Good Pharmacy Practice Education) 

GPPEd(Good Pharmacy Practice Education) on the other hand, is more centered on pharmacy education and aims to improve it via practical learning. This involves making budding pharmacists more aware of their role and their country’s needs so that after graduating, they can make practical use of their education by providing better services. GPPed Program and Trainers Development Camp (TDC) are programs introduced by the International Pharmaceutical Students’ Federation Asia Pacific Regional Office (IPSF APRO) and IPSF.

9th APRO Good Pharmacy Practice Education [GPPed , Nepal]

The 9th APRO GPPed Program will be hosted by Fop, Nepal themed “From Classroom to practice” . The theme "from classroom to practice" underscores the importance of bridging the gap between theoretical learning and practical application in the field of pharmacy. It emphasizes the transition that pharmacy students or professionals need to make from the academic setting to real-life scenarios, where they must apply their acquired knowledge, practical skills, and ethical principles effectively. This theme highlights the significance of hands-on experience and experiential learning in shaping competent and ethical pharmacy practitioners who can navigate the complexities of healthcare settings and contribute positively to patient care outcomes. It encourages students and professionals to reflect on their classroom learning experiences and integrate them into their practice, ultimately enhancing the quality and effectiveness of pharmaceutical care delivery.

OBJECTIVES

  • The main objective of this symposium is to implement and promote good pharmacy practice by training and developing leaders along with establishing pharmacy practice guidelines so that a consistent standard is reflected across not just a country or APRO Region but among different countries, internationally.
  • This event will bring regulatory bodies, educational institutions, organizations, speakers, and participants, international delegates together to discuss and highlight the recent development of Good Pharmacy Practice and to further emphasize on developing its implementation in the most efficient manner.
  • This program will endeavor to help students gain knowledge about the current situation of the good pharmacy practice in Nepal and other Asia Pacific countries and its implementation.

VISA INFORMATION

Tribhuvan International Airport is the only international airport of Nepal. Immigration Office, TIA (Tribhuvan International Airport) under the Department of Immigration has been facilitating tourists flying to Nepal by providing a Visa on Arrival. 'On Arrival' visa procedure is very quick and simple. You can expect some queues during peak Tourist season. If you wish to skip those queues, you can also consider getting a Visa from Nepalese Diplomatic Missions stationed abroad prior to your arrival. Choice is yours.

If you have obtained a visa from Nepalese Diplomatic Missions, then you must enter Nepal within six months from the visa issued date. Your total stay is counted starting from the day you enter Nepal.

Visas obtained on Arrival at the Entry and Exit points are 'Tourist Visas'.  They bear multiple Re-entry facilities. Tourist Visa 'On Arrival' is the only entry visa to Nepal. If you are visiting Nepal for purposes other than Tourism (sightseeing, tour, travel, mountaineering, trekking, visiting friends and families), you should still get a ' Tourist Visa' to get into the country.  However, you must change the category of visa as per your purpose and length of stay in Nepal from the Department of Immigration by producing required documents.

Nationals of the following countries are requested to acquire a Visa prior to their arrival from their nearby Diplomatic missions (Embassies/consulates) of Nepal Government.

Please follow these simple procedures for Tourist Visa on Arrival at the airport (TIA)  if you have not acquired a Visa prior to boarding the plane.

1st  Step

Fill in ' Arrival Card '

Fill in the Online ‘Tourist Visa ‘form ( you can fill it up  prior to your arrival  visiting our official website  Department of Immigration / fill it up using Kiosk machines upon your arrival at the airport).  If you fill it from the website, you will get a submission Receipt with barcode, please print it out and bring it along for acquiring a visa. It works for fifteen days and becomes invalid then after.  If so, you will have to fill it up again.

2nd  Step  

Make payment  at the bank according to your visa requirement ( 15/30/90 Days)

Get the receipt

While you can use different modes of payments (at visa fees collection counter), we advise you to carry some cash to be on the safe side.

On Arrival Visa Fee at Entry Points

15 Days – 30 USD

30 Days – 50 USD

90 Days – 125 USD

3rd Step  

Proceed to the Immigration Desk with your online form,  payment receipts and your passport

Hand in your documents to immigration officer for visa processing. He/she issues visa to you upon his/her satisfaction.

Visa Extension Fee

Tourist visa extension is done for a minimum 15 days with USD 45 and USD 3 per day for additional days.

In the case of delay less than 150 days additional USD 5 per day as late fine.

Gratis Visa (Visa for Free)

Gratis Visa is issued free of cost in case of following categories of Visa applicants:

Children below 10 years except US citizens

Up to 30 days for SAARC Citizens (except Afghanistan) visiting Nepal for the first time in a given visa Year.  Afghan citizens are eligible for Gratis Visa on Arrival only upon the recommendation of the Department of Immigration. If you are an Afghan citizen, you can request a concerned institution inviting you to Nepal for necessary paperwork with the Department of Immigration to get you Gratis Visa 'On Arrival'.

Non Residential Nepalese(NRN) card holder ( issued by MoFA /Nepalese diplomatic missions abroad)

Chinese Nationals

Officials from China, Brazil, Russia and Thailand do not need Entry Visa based on reciprocal visa waiver Agreement

Visas of all kinds including ‘Gratis’ issued at the Airport are Tourist Visas. Contact the Department of Immigration for extending your visa or changing the category of your visa. Tourist Visa extension can be done from Immigration Office, Pokhara too. Non tourist visa extension can be done only at the Department of Immigration (if eligible) for a period of maximum one year (except business visa).

Educational visit

  1. Godawari Botanical garden

Discover the NBG, once the Royal Botanical Garden, now under Nepal's Department of Plant Resources. Crafted by British architects, it's a hub for plant conservation, research, and recreation. Spread across 82 hectares at the foot of Mt. Phulchowki, with 40 hectares dedicated to themed gardens. Nestled amidst lush evergreen forests, with the enchanting Godawari stream flowing through its heart. Explore this botanical paradise! Click on the link for more about this: https://nbg.gov.np/

  1. HRDC

In the heart of compassion blooms a sanctuary, a haven where hope intertwines with healing—the Hospital and Rehabilitation Centre for Disabled Children(HRDC). Here, amidst the bustling streets of Kathmandu, lies a beacon of light, founded by the visionary Prof. Ashok K. Banskota and nurtured by the altruistic spirit of the Friends of Disabled. For twenty-six years, this sacred place has stood as a bastion of care, embracing physically disabled children aged sixteen and under, cradling them in warmth and understanding. Click on this link to know more: https://www.hrdcnepal.org/

  1. Pharmaceutical Industry

  Nepal's pharmaceutical industry is a whirlwind of innovation and progress! From cities to villages, companies are delivering life-saving medicines with boundless energy. Despite challenges, they're unstoppable, shaping the future of healthcare with quality and affordability. Get ready for a thrilling ride as Nepal's pharmaceutical pioneers lead the way to a healthier tomorrow and join us for an educational visit to one of these dynamic industries and witness the magic firsthand!
